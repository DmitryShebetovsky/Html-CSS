In the year 1878 I took my degree of Doctaor of Medicine of the University of London, and proceeded to Netley to go throught the course prescribed for surgeons in the army. Having comleted my studoes there, I was duly attached to the First Northumberland Fusiliers as Assistant Surgeon. The regiment was stationed in India at the time, and before I could join it, the second Afghan war had had broken out. On landing at Bombay. I learned that my corps had advanced through the passes, and was already deep in the enemy's country.
